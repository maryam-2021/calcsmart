📝 In-Depth Guide: Mastering Roman Numeral Converter

Roman numerals originated in ancient Rome and remained the standard way of writing numbers throughout Europe well into the late Middle Ages. Today, they are still used for clock faces, book chapters, movie sequels, and sporting events like the Super Bowl.

How to Read Roman Numerals

Roman numerals are based on seven symbols. When a smaller symbol appears after a larger one, you add them (VI = 5 + 1 = 6). When a smaller symbol appears before a larger one, you subtract it (IV = 5 - 1 = 4).

The Standard Symbols

  • I: 1
  • V: 5
  • X: 10
  • L: 50
  • C: 100
  • D: 500
  • M: 1,000

Common Constraints

In the standard modern style, you shouldn't use more than three of the same symbol in a row (e.g., 4 is IV, not IIII). This restricts the standard Roman numeral system to numbers between 1 and 3,999.
